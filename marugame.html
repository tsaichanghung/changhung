Marugame Seimen mobile ordering service

" A more simple, clear, and content-focused experience on Marugame Seimen mobile ordering service. "

Project Overview

2020.10~2021.04
Objective
To respond to the increasing needs of takeout and order-ahead during the pandemic of Covid-19, We redesigned the existing order experience to enhance both the usability and the utility.
Deliverables
As a product designer, I was responsible for both the research and design, and went through variable tasks, including working closely with team members to discover and define the problems in the early stage, visualizing concepts and presenting design works to clients during the design phase, falicitating an overall formative evaluation and reporting the quantitative result to relevant stakeholders.
Chanllenge
In an ideal world, we would have dived into user research at the beginning, but the business was not willing to fund the time and effort involved. Besides, there are a good few of technical dependencies on the current sysytem, so we needed to deal with many prioritizations to to meet the schedule.
Outcome
1) The redesign successfully enhanced the efficiency of purchase in comparison of the existing one by saving 1/3 time while ordering items. 2) The repeat rate has increased by 40% since the redesign released on 2021/8/26. 3) I accompolished the while design process on time and it became a benchmark in our design team.

Background

Marugame Seimen (TORIDOLL), the well known chain store in Japan, released a mobile ordering service in early 2020. Based on the O:der Pick-up Plaftform, users can order and set a pick up time ahead to save the time and skip the queues, especially during the pandemic of Covid-19. However, when the clients kept rolling out new combo menus to meet customers’ needs, the current version became cluttered and unfriendly, because O:der Pick-up Platform provided a general ordering UI, but not a optimal one.

Research

In the early stage of this project, the specific requirement about UI from our client was: Improve the experience of ordering a Combo menu.

On the other hand, there were some existing and realistc issues on the current system: the technology was quite outedated, and Marugame Seimen should isolate from the pick-up platform to relieve the effort from our customer service team as well.

Based on those known issues, it was an opportunity to review and improve the current service, and it was worth establishing a new benchmark as well for any further plan, such as iteratively enhancement by data-driven process, because the active users might be huge.

After some discussions with PM, engineers, customer support members, we tended to make an overhall rather than just an incremental change, and planed to sync with our clien to seek an understanding.

I decided to strengthen domain knownledge of the current state before progressing to any hypothetical while making a plan.

🤔 What do users acutally behave when they try to achieve their goals?

🤔 How do users think when they try to complete any task during the order process?

To get answers to these two questions, I conducted formative usability testing. Meanwhile, I identified the core problems existing in the ordering flow. The followings are what we learned from 7 participants:

A part of narrative (As-Is Journey Map) of ordering udon on the current version

🧐 Problem

  • 不要な情報・要素を積み重ねていくゆえに、被験者の認知負荷とタスクの所要時間が増加

    Unnecessary informaiton and UI will dramatically increase not only the cognitive effort but also the time when users want to complete their orders.

  • 曖昧なラベルと不自然な目線の動きによりユーザが情報を見落とししたり、誤操作をしったりしがち

    Unclear indication and wordings will make users misunderstand and lead them to a wrong place.

💡 Insight

  • ユーザーが効率よくオーダーを捗りたい

    Users wants to complete the order process as soon as possible, such as "Acquire informaiton", "Compare some target items" or "make a decision", etc. Effciency would be the key metrix and principle of the the redesign

  • リスクを避けるため、重要な画面を遷移する際に、スマホの物理キーよりUI上の要素に操作に頼りたい

    Users are tend to tap the specific link or navigation rather than to use the physical functional key on mobile, such as back to the somethere when the cart isn't empty.

  • 複数商品を注文するケースに、被験者が決まっている順番で商品を選んでいく傾向が見られる

    Most of users are tend to select more than one item, and they are used to choose the main udon first, then choose side menu or topping if desired.

Define

To ensure any solutions proposed later are desirable to users, viable for the organization, and feasible with the technology made available, I worked closely with PM and engineers, prioritizing all the usability issues gathered and aimed to clearly outline the scope of this project.

After several intensive discussions about the costs and limitations with relevant internal stakeholders, finally, we defined the design scope of this project and formulated some design principles that gave us more clear goals when we ideate a resolution or evaluate it

To-Be Journey Map

ideation & iteration

I conducted the design works with several iterations to make sure whether all the hypotheses followed the design principles or not. Here are some deliverables:

探索しすい、発見しやすい・一覧性のあるメニューリスト画面

How will users choose their first menu?

  • With a pre-determined goal:
    1. Quickly scan and recognize what the interface and elements mean
    2. Find the relative section
    3. Scroll and look around the target
    4. Focus on images, then check the name of menu
    5. Find and confirm the target item
  • Seek some inspiration:
    1. Explore from top to bottom of the menu to gain the informaiton
    2. At the same time, some of the items that catched his/her eyes during scrolling down
    3. Compare and make a decision

Based on user's behavior and mindset, I came up with some concepts that might support both two kinds of users to complete their task more efficiently:

Provide essential and noticeable informaiton within the first-view as possible as we can

Menu informaton is what users desire to gain when they enter this page.

"How long should I wait?" should noticeable and display in the early stage of whole task instead of placing in the later step.

Make a menu list that ease to browse and manipulate

The interface can lead users find and compare target items easily.

Fig. 1 UI of V6 (old version)

気付き・理解・操作しやいインターフェース

When users enter the menu detail page, they often quickly scan the image and name (even description) for just a little bit more comfirmation. The main purpose and task would be understanding how to order if there are some options, changing the quantity. After confirming the price, they will expect that there is a clear accessable way to bring them to go forward. That is the CTA of this page: add to cart.

Make CTA always be displayed near the eye gazing path. To prevent users from getting confused when they are trying to go forward.

Strengthen the role of navigation element more intuitive.With placing a little left arrow at the left of the link which labelled ”お品書き画面へ”, the motivation of "back to menu list" is quickly connected to the action.

セットメニューの操作最適化

Rather than display all the required options without ordering, as a typothesis, I constructured and defined the flow of combo menu, then validated whether it match the mental model that existing in users' brain.

The comparison of Combo menu: Before (left) and After (right)

より明確的な・安心的なオーダー体験

Setting a proper pickup time is rather important on this service because it represents a sign of when should start to prepare the meal and the deadline of the preparation task. During the usability testing, I found that almost of the participants didn't change the pickup time. Here were some reasons from them:

  • I did check the pickup time, and the default pickup time is good for me.
  • Oh, actually I didn't read it, I mean, I've never been aware of the pickup time.

I could be an crucial problem that caused they place an order that they can't pickup on time actually or an unitended pickup time.

Noticeable and clear pickup time information. By giving a particular visual weight of pickup time information and emphasizing the "The earliest estimated pickup time" by red color, I orginaized a new layout which would be ease to focus on and ease to understand the current state and make a decision.

Predictable and less cogonitive load. State the rule clearly and display the supporting text underneath the input rather than as a placeholder to avoid memorizing.

ライティング・言い回し

I've learned from the previous user research that users tended to omit some copies and respond quickly depending on if their mental model was strong enough or not. That means it could be more concise and help users focus on the content. However, sometimes users will feel slightly confused even if they knew there was only one way to go forward. And I solved this kind of problems by conducting several user testing to discover their mental model.

Concise and communicative copy. Tell users the only important things with visual weight.

Action-oriented copy. Trigger users' motivation and emotion by specific object and navigate users to where they should go with predictable words.

Evaluate

Since the primary goal of this project was improve the efficiency of the existing ordering experience. Does it work? I conducted an evaluation both on the redesign and the old one to compare the efficiency, which includes:

How much time would be saved while ordering on the redesign? How fast will users complete the ordering task compare with the current version?

To answer the question above, I ran a summative usability testing both on existing one and the redesign.

Comparing with the existing version, here comes the significant outcome when 32 participants completed the tasks on the redesign as below:

1/3
⏱TIME SAVED
3/4
👍ERRORS* REDUCED

* Errors is refered to 1) Slips and 2) Mistakes

Summary of this evaluation

  • Goal
    1. Assess the time on task of "Choose and add items to cart" on both the redesign and old version
    2. Assess the accuracy of "Choose and add items to cart" on both the redesign and old version
    3. Check the whole ordering usability on the redesign
  • Participants
    1. 32 (two of them was in pilot testing, 27 of them have pioir experience of the old site)
  • Methodology
    1. Usability testing
    2. Within-subject design (all of the participants went through the task on new and old one but with different order: half of participants went from new to old, while the others went the opposite order)
  • Task
    1. "Order four items which exist in different four kinds of categories and add them to the cart"
    2. "Imagine you feel like go for Marugame for lunch (or dinner, depends on when the test took place) first. What would you want to eat? Now you can order-ahead and complete the payment on your smartphone, and pick it up later without wasting your time, please order what you want and tell me if you think done."
  • Result
    1. Average time on task1: Redesign: 55 sec. The Old site: 78 sec.
    2. 100% of participants complete the task1 on the redersign faster than on the old one (even most of them have piror experience on the old site)　
    3. Accuracy*: Redesign→ Level3: 100%; the old site→ Level3: 23%, Level2: 53%, Level1: 23%
    4. Satisfation: Redesign > the old site: 27; Redesign ≥ the old site: 3

*Definition of Accuracy in this study: Level3: Zero time waste (or only slip one time); Level2: Repeatedly slips on the same step; Level1: Many mistakes happen and waste much time for the unintentional action.

Increase repeat rate by 40%. The repeat rate has dramatically inceased by 40% since the countrywide release on 2021/8/26. Currently there are 621 stores stably operating everyday in Japan.
